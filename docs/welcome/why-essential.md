# Why Essential?

## **The Problem with Today's Blockchains**

Blockchain technology has traditionally relied on **imperative programming**—a model where users must specify *how* to achieve a result through explicit, step-by-step instructions. This approach turns blockchains into complex instruction manuals, forcing users to manage execution details rather than focusing on desired outcomes. As a result, transactions become slow, costly, and often confusing.

These limitations lead to:

- **High fees**
- **Centralized control by middlemen**
- **Unpredictable results**
- **Frustrating user experience**

Simply put, traditional blockchains aren't built with the user in mind.

## **Embracing Intents with Declarative Programming**

**Declarative programming** shifts the focus from *how* to *what*. Instead of detailing every step to achieve a goal, users simply state their intended outcome, known as an **intent**. This simplifies interactions and aligns technology more closely with user needs.

However, current intent-based systems often revert to imperative execution when settling transactions on the blockchain, limiting the full potential of declarative principles.

**Example:**

- **Declarative (Intent):** "I will pay $20 for a Hawaiian pizza delivered to my door by 8 PM."
- **Imperative (Transaction):** "Get on your scooter, go to Domino's, order a Hawaiian pizza (item #4), and bring it to my address by 8 PM. Here's $18 for the pizza and $2 for gas."

At Essential, we're flipping the script with a **declarative blockchain** model.

## **Why Essential Changes the Game**

By integrating declarative principles at its core, Essential's blockchain allows users to submit intents—clear statements of what they want—and lets the network handle *how* to achieve them. This eliminates the need for explicit instructions or reverting to imperative methods.

It's time to move beyond the limitations of imperative programming and embrace a declarative future that aligns with the true potential of decentralized technologies.

- **Better for Users:** Predictable, reliable outcomes enhance trust and simplify the user experience.
- **Better for Developers:** Reduced complexity in coding and deployment allows for faster innovation and fewer errors.
- **Better for Web3:** Unlocks true scalability, lowers transaction costs, and promotes a more decentralized and efficient ecosystem.

By fully embracing declarative principles, we can overcome the inherent limitations of imperative blockchains. This shift enables a more intuitive, efficient, and user-centric Web3, where technology adapts to human intentions rather than forcing humans to adapt to technological constraints.
